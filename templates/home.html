Introduction to Areca Leaf Diseases

Areca nut cultivation plays an important role in agriculture. However, areca plants are highly affected by fungal and bacterial infections, which reduce crop yield and quality.

1. Leaf Spot Disease

Leaf spot disease is caused mainly by fungal pathogens. It appears as small brown or black circular spots on the leaf surface. Over time, these spots enlarge and may merge, causing leaf drying. High humidity and poor air circulation increase the spread of this disease.

2. Yellow Leaf Disease

Yellow leaf disease causes gradual yellowing of leaves starting from the tips. The plant becomes weak and growth reduces. This disease is often associated with nutrient deficiency and microbial infection.

3. Healthy Leaf

A healthy areca leaf is uniformly green in color, free from spots, patches, or discoloration. Healthy leaves indicate proper nutrient levels and disease-free growth.

This project uses a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model to automatically detect whether an uploaded areca leaf image is Healthy or affected by a specific disease.
